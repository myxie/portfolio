I am currently undertaking my honours dissertation in Computer Science at the University of Western Australia, with a focus on the applications of scheduling algorithms in SKA-scale data processing pipelines. My research interests include algorithmic theory and design, artificial intelligence, systems programming, and computer science education.

Currently, I am a Teaching Assistant for the Bachelor of Philosophy (Honours) program, where I help facilitate workshops and marks assignments for the Global Challenges, Research and Leadership unit. I am also involved in educational outreach programs with the International Centre for Radio Astronomy Research, where I teach primary school students about computing and astronomy, and develop web tools to help teachers create their own resources.

How To Play Computer Games: This is a paper that introduces the reader to the concept of Game-playing algorithms, and discusses recent developments in approaching the problem.

Big Data Frameworks for Radio Astronomy Data: A Summary: This Literature Review draws upon developments across three major Big Data frameworks, and assesses their viability for large-scale science projects like the Square Kilometer Array.

Comparing Rank Methods in HEFT Scheduling: This is a comparison paper that investigates the ranking methods used in the Heterogeneous Earliest-Finish-Time algorithm.
